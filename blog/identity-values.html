Identity Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.My biggest ethical flex was a recent decision I made for my own health. The values I had to weigh up was a nice steady income, or recovering from a concussion and getting a handle on my PTSD. I had tried for months to make everything work in concert, but it was obvious I needed to take a back seat for a bit. I chose my health and it is one of my proudest decisions.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity. Even though my immediate family has had a huge impact on who I am, I would like to talk about the impact my chosen family has had. They have taught me loyalty, the value of hard work, resilience, and kindness. Without the fortitude of character I have around me in my best friends, I would be lost.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey. I approach most things with humour. Sometimes too much and it can get a bit unserious. But I think it works in my favour as a stress reliever. My other strengths are my creativity, which I think is the backbone of problem solving. In the modern age we have so many different avenues we can use to solve problems, and I think all of them have their time and place. Something else that might help me is my ability to hyperfixate on tasks hahahaha.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey? My health again, is a big one. And while my ability to hyperfixate can help me, it can also mean I forget to take breaks, leave things until the last minute, and forget to eat. If I'm being very honest about my flaws right now. My disabilities have been both a hindrance and a blessing, but during this journey I would like to learn to be proactive and manage my time wisely. I believe I can do it. Sprint 2 hasn't been the greatest in that regard but I'm resilient and will keep trying until I strike the balance. I want to meet every deadline. By the end of Foundations I want to have planned and developed a good routine to facilitate these dreams.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why. In University I had a really embarrassing moment that still makes me cringe now. I completely ghosted a group project and got flamed by the professor for it. I gave no notice of what was going on with me, and I was too shy to talk to the other members of my group. I've learnt a lot from that experience. 1. Never ghost (in a professional environment, unless there is abject disrespect from the other party, in that case I support ghosting), 2. ALWAYS give appropriate and timely notice of what's going awry, and 3. try to see the situation from the other party's perspective.
